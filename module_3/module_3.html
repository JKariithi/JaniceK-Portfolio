Introduction to ESP32 and Electrical circuits and Components

Electronic Circuits and Components

An electronic circuit is a structure that directs and controls electric current to perform various functions.

The simplest circuit has these three key components

  • Conducting path: Where the current flows. Copper wires are used for this but they are being replaced by conductive traces.
  • Voltage Source:Provides a potential differences between two points in the circuit. It is a two terminal device such as batteries, genetators or power systems
  • Load: Consumes power to perform a particular function. Light bulb is the simplest example.

Types of Electronic Circuits

  1. Open Circuit

    2. Where the current can't flow as one or two components are disconnected intentionally using a switch or accidentally.

  2. Closed Circuit

    3. Where the circuit forms a complete loop with no interruptions. This is regardless if it performs or doesn't perform a circuit.

  3. Short Circuit

    4. This is low resistance connection forms between two points in an electronic circuit. As a result the current tends to flow through this newly formed connection rather than along the intended path. They are however dangerous and lead to serious accidents.

  4. Printed Circuit Boards(PCBs)

    5. A layout of a cicuit printed chemically onto a plastic board. Mostly used in complex electronic circuits are required.

  5. Intergrated Circuits

    6. Tiny electronic circuits that can fit inside a small chip. Main purpose is to increase effeciency of the electronic devices while reducing manufacturing cost.

Electronic Components

Capacitor

How it works: Stores and releases electrical energy by accumulating charge on two plates separated by an insulator.

Function: Temporary energy storage, filtering, smoothing voltage.

Applications: Power supply filters, timing circuits, signal coupling/decoupling.

Resistors

How it works: Resists the flow of electric current, converting electrical energy into heat.

Function: Limits current, divides voltage, sets biasing conditions.

Applications: LED current limiting, voltage dividers, pull-up/pull-down in digital circuits.

Diode

How it works:Allows current to flow in only one direction (forward-biased) and blocks it in the opposite (reverse-biased).

Function: Rectification, protection against reverse polarity, signal demodulation

Applications: Power rectifiers, voltage clamping, LED lights..

Transistor

How it works: Acts as a switch or amplifier using a small input current to control a larger output current.

Function: Signal amplification, switching.

Applications: Amplifiers, digital logic gates, microcontrollers.

Indcutor

How it works: Stores energy in a magnetic field when current flows through it; opposes changes in current..

Function: Energy storage, filtering, inductive coupling.

Applications: Power supplies, RF circuits, transformers.

Relay

How it works: Electromagnetic switch that opens/closes contacts when a control current is applied.

Function: Isolates and switches high-voltage or high-current circuits using low-power control signals.

Applications: Automation systems, motor control, safety circuits.

Quartz Crystal

How it works: Vibrates at a precise frequency when electrically stimulated (piezoelectric effect)

Function: Provides accurate timing and frequency reference.

Applications: Clocks, microcontrollers, communication devices.
